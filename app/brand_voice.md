# Illapa — Brand Voice Guidelines

**Three registers in one voice: pedagogical, reverent, professional.**

## Tone

- **Pedagogical** — we teach earthquake engineering to LATAM students. Every explanation starts from first principles. No jargon without a definition.
- **Reverent** — *Illapa* is an Inca deity. The naming is not decoration — it anchors the framework in Andean cultural memory. Treat mythology with respect; never exoticize.
- **Professional** — engineers will judge the numerics. Be precise, cite codes (NTE E.030-2018, ASCE 41-17), disclose limitations openly.

## Voice principles

1. Honest disclosure over narrative polish.
2. Bilingual by default: Spanish primary, English secondary — LATAM reads Spanish first.
3. Numbers with units, always.
4. Cite the code clause (e.g. "E.030 Art. 28.2"), never a vague "per the code".

## Example sentences

**ES (primary):**
- "Illapa es un laboratorio de ingeniería sísmica reproducible, no una herramienta de diseño comercial."
- "Curva de fragilidad calculada con 62 registros sintéticos Kanai-Tajimi por nivel de PGA."
- "El modelo shear-type subestima la demanda — documentado, no corregido."

**EN (secondary):**
- "Illapa is a reproducible earthquake-engineering lab — not a production design tool."
- "Fragility curve computed from 62 Kanai-Tajimi synthetic records per PGA level."
- "The shear-type model underestimates demand — documented, not corrected."

## Words to avoid

- Marketing hype: *cutting-edge, revolutionary, groundbreaking, game-changing, next-generation, disruptive, seamless, unleash, empower*.
- Empty intensifiers: *very, really, super, incredibly*.
- False authority: *industry-leading, world-class, best-in-class*.
- Vague code references: *per the code, according to standards* — always cite the specific clause.
